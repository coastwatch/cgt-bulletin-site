Bottom Line

CGT is still strategically investable, but the market now rewards execution far more than novelty.

Five things leadership should know

  • Regulatory outcomes remain uneven. Rocket’s Kresladi approval shows pathways still exist, while Regenxbio’s rejection shows FDA scrutiny remains high.
  • Manufacturing has become central to value creation. It now directly affects approval timing, product availability, investor confidence, and launch pacing.
  • Big pharma still wants high-quality CGT assets. Gilead/Arcellx and AstraZeneca’s continued expansion support this.
  • China is becoming structurally important. Not just for assets, but for manufacturing footprint, competition, and strategic positioning.
  • Commercial proof exists, but scale-up is still hard. Casgevy and other examples show that approved therapies still face operational friction.

What this means for the market

  • The sector is shifting from platform hype to operational discipline.
  • Cell therapy sentiment is generally stronger than broad gene therapy sentiment.
  • Differentiated assets still command capital; weaker stories are punished quickly.
  • Partners who reduce execution risk are becoming more valuable.

Most important conclusion

The next winners in CGT will not just have compelling science. They will have better evidence, cleaner manufacturing, stronger regulatory packages, and more executable commercial models.
